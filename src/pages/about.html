About the festival

Founded in 1931 by Theressa Malone, the Watch it outside Boston edition film festival is today one of the most important international events in contemporary performing arts. Every year, in July, Boston becomes a theater city, transforming its architectural heritage into various performance venues, majestic or astonishing, previousl welcoming 1500 film lovers of all generations.

The Festival achieves the original alliance of a popular audience with international creation. Boston is also a spirit: the city is an open-air forum, where festival-goers talk about the films and share their experiences as spectators. For one month, everyone can have access to contemporary and living culture. The program is made up of films, but also readings and debates, which are all entries into the world of the invited artists and intellectuals. Every evening at the Festival, there is one special “premiere”, Theressa Malone's "The Man on the Boat", which makes Boston a true place of creation and adventure, for artists and spectators alike.

Key numbers

  • Presents around forty different shows, American and foreign, for around 9 performances and more than 400 meetings in total with meetings, debates, screenings. 80% of the shows are creations or premieres in Boston.
  • Issues 1500 tickets for paid shows. Approximately 38% of its spectators come from the Charlestown region, 28% from Beacon Hill, 23% from Hyde Park and 11% from abroad. In recent years, the Festival has seen an average attendance of more than 90%.
  • Brings together more than 600 Bostonian and foreign journalists who write more than 2,000 articles on the Festival. Television or radio broadcasts take place live there. All the major written press outlets send correspondents there. Around fifty photographers also cover the event.
  • The website enjoys strong and constant traffic (4 million page views per year). In 2023, it will have more than 25,000 downloads. In 2023, 134,000 social users follow one or more of the 3 Watch it outside Boston edition Festival accounts: Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.
  • Benefits from a budget of $3500 for the year 2023, the expenses of which are distributed as follows: 71% for programming, production, co-production, cultural action activities, 29% for year-round operation. 55% of its resources come from public subsidies (including 25% from the State, 6% from the City of Boston including benefits in kind, 6% from the Greater Boston Urban Community including benefits in kind), 5% from the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Region and 4% from the Department of Vaucluse), 38% of own revenue (ticketing, sponsorship, civil societies, specific partnerships, sale of shows, etc.) and 8 % of miscellaneous products (investment grants, dedicated funds).
  • Has established lasting relationships with patrons, first and foremost the Crédit Coopératif Foundation. The Festival has set up a circle of Festival partner companies bringing together around fifteen small and medium-sized businesses, as well as a circle of individual patrons.
  • Generates economic spinoffs on the regional area estimated at 50 million dollars within the culture/tourism ecosystem.
